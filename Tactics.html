Fight Psychic Scams
Start by Recognizing the Tricks First.

In psychic and fortune-telling scams, individuals claim to have special powers to predict the future, communicate with a deceased loved one, or provide insights into a person's life. These scams prey on vulnerable people, particularly those who are grieving, lonely or struggling financially. The scammers solicit business through mail, social media platforms, storefronts, and even street encounters. While there are common and legitimate services such as palm reading, tarot reading, or astrological advice offering entertainment opportunities or general guidance, there are many con artists and fraudsters who exploit the industry.

The following are the common fraud techniques and methods used by fraud criminals:
COLD READING, HOT READING, WARM READING, THE BARNUM EFFECT

COLD READING



Cold reading is a psychological manipulation technique often used in fortune-telling, psychic readings, and fraudulent spiritual practices to convince a person that the reader has deep, personal insights into their life. Unlike "hot reading," which relies on pre-gathered information, cold reading operates without prior knowledge, leveraging a mix of psychological cues, statistical probabilities, and verbal tricks to create the illusion of supernatural perception.

Psychological Mechanisms Behind Cold Reading



The Forer Effect (Barnum Effect) People tend to accept vague, general statements as highly accurate for themselves, especially when these statements are presented as personalized insights. Confirmation Bias Subjects often focus on statements that seem accurate while ignoring or rationalizing incorrect ones, reinforcing the perception of the reader's accuracy. Social Desirability Bias Individuals often subconsciously cooperate with the reader, wanting the experience to feel meaningful, which leads them to provide confirming details without realizing it.

HOT READING



Hot reading is a deceptive tactic used by fraudulent fortune-tellers, psychics, and spiritual mediums to create the illusion of supernatural insight. Unlike cold reading, which relies on making general guesses and reading physical cues without prior knowledge, hot reading involves gathering specific, often highly personal information about a target before the actual reading takes place. This pre-collected data can make the predictions seem astonishingly accurate, reinforcing the scammer’s credibility and encouraging trust.

Key Psychological Mechanisms



The Illusion of Omniscience -- Hot reading creates a powerful psychological effect by making the target believe the psychic possesses supernatural knowledge, reinforcing their emotional dependence.

Confirmation Bias -- Once a person believes in a psychic’s abilities, they are more likely to overlook inaccuracies and focus on the “hits,” further entrenching their belief in the scam.

Emotional Vulnerability -- Many victims of hot reading scams are emotionally vulnerable, often seeking comfort after a loss or during a major life transition, making them more susceptible to this form of manipulation.

WARM READING



Warm reading is a psychological manipulation technique used in fortune-telling, psychic readings, and other forms of spiritual fraud. It falls between the highly improvisational nature of cold reading and the premeditated approach of hot reading. Unlike cold reading, which starts without any prior information, and hot reading, which relies on specific pre-gathered facts, warm reading exploits broadly applicable psychological insights and socially common experiences to create a sense of accuracy and connection.

Warm reading is particularly effective because it blends universally relatable statements with the careful observation of the subject’s reactions, making it seem as if the reader has special insight into the client’s life.

How Warm Reading Works



Exploiting Universal Human Experiences:
Warm readers often rely on themes that resonate broadly, such as family dynamics, personal ambitions, and common fears.

Playing on the Desire for Validation:
Many people seek comfort, encouragement, or validation during psychic readings. Warm readers use this to their advantage by offering positive affirmations

Leveraging Demographic and Cultural Norms:
Warm readers often make educated guesses based on a client’s age, gender, clothing, accent, or cultural background.

THE BARNUM EFFECT



The Barnum Effect, also known as the Forer Effect, is a psychological phenomenon where individuals believe that vague, general statements about personality are highly accurate for them personally. This effect is often exploited in psychic readings, horoscopes, and fortune-telling scams to create the illusion of personal insight.

People tend to accept these statements as true, especially when they are presented as tailored specifically for them.

Psychological Mechanisms Behind the Barnum Effect



The Forer Effect: People accept vague, general statements as highly accurate for themselves, especially when presented as personalized.

Confirmation Bias: Individuals focus on statements that seem accurate and ignore those that do not, reinforcing the illusion of accuracy.

Social Desirability Bias: People may subconsciously cooperate with the reader, providing confirming details and making the experience feel more meaningful.
